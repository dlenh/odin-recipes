Spicy Cucumber Salad

Description

Spicy cucumber salad, also known as oi muchim, is a popular side dish in Korean culture. Fresh cucumbers are marinated in a mixture of vinegar, red pepper flakes, soy sauce, sugar, along with other ingredients. They retain their crunchiness, make for a refreshing appetizer, and pair well with rice or noodles.

All of the ingredients can be found at your local Korean market. If you cannot find Korean cucumbers, a variety of different cucumbers, like English, Japanese, or Persian, works for this recipe as well. Gochugaru, coarsely ground Korean red chili pepper flakes, usually comes in big bags, but it can be used in other delicious recipes.

The secret to retaining the cucumbers' crunchiness is by using salt to draw out their natural moisture content. The other ingredients then create a flavor profile that is spicy, tangy, and a bit sweet to complement the crunch.

Ingredients

  • 1 Korean cucumber
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 2 teaspoons rice wine vinegar
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 green onion, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil
  • 2 teaspoons sesame seeds, toasted

Steps

  1. Slice the cucumber to desired thickness (1/4 to 1/2-inch thick). Toss gently with salt and rice wine vinegar and set aside for 15-30 minutes. This pickling method will draw out the cucumbers' moisture content.
  2. Rinse the cucumbers under running water and try a piece. Continue rinsing until desired level of saltiness. Remember that soy sauce, another source of sodium, will be added later.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix together gochugaru, sugar, soy sauce, minced garlic, chopped green onions, sesame oil, and toasted sesame seeds.
  4. Toss cucumbers with dressing until well-coated.
  5. Enjoy!
