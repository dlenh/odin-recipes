Spicy Rice Cake

Description

Tteokbokki literally translates to stir-fried rice cake. Rice cakes are made from glutinous rice pounded into different shapes and sizes. This dish can use any shape, and is an extremely popular street food in Korean. The rice cake is chewy and the bright, red sauce is warming with a bit of heat from the gochujang (Korean red chili pepper paste) and gochugaru (Korean red chili flake).

Other ingredients such as fishcake, seafood, cabbage, ramen, hot dog, egg, and cheese can be added to increase the complexity of the dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound rice cake, fresh or frozen
  • 2 cups water
  • 3 tablespoons gochujang
  • 1 teaspoon gochugaru
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce

Steps

  1. If you are using frozen rice cakes, soak them in cold water and drain once they are soft.
  2. In a saucepan over medium high heat, bring to a boil water, gochujang, gochugaru, sugar, and soy sauce.
  3. Add the rice cakes, lower the heat, and let simmer covered with a lid for 8-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the lid and let simmer until the sauce thickens.
  5. Enjoy!
